NEXT Stop: a new service designed for HSL

Next Stop is the result of 6 weeks effort in Aalto service camp, to develope a new service for HSL.

HSl was looking to attract more customers by having variety of new and digital services. After having brain storming sessions and some primirly interviews with customers, we noticed that many people have problem with getting accurate information to plan their daily trips. Moreover, we realized for tourists, public transpportation is not an option mostly

because they find it difficult to plan their trips with lack of information and so much uncertainity.

So We introduce Next step as a solution. Next Stop creates a rapid, entertaining and flexible way to plan your micro trips. We are targeting tourists to help them get the most out of their day when they are exploring a new city.

PROBLEM:

During our interviews we found out that when it comes to travelling people might have different attitude for planning or exploring the city, some are spontaneous and some, although not interested in a school time schedule, still prefer to have at least a vague plan, but one thing they all have in common is to enjoy their trip with the least frustration of thinking about where to go? how to go there? what places is similar to their style or mood and how to enjoy every single moment of their trip?

When it comes to planning your trips, it takes a lot of time, it is often not fun and once you’ve come up with your plan, it's inflexible.

Interviews

You probably know already what the traditional tourist guides tell a tourist, for example in Helsinki: Visit the Stone Church in Temppelinaukio, go see the Sibelius Monument, and so on… is that why we really travel? To see only the surface and see only what other tourists see? Or would you want to go deeper and get better recommendations, experience new things from different perspectives? The fact is, culture has been changing and many people are looking for real connections with local and experiencing a new city in a more real yet exciting way.

Market Analysis:

What unites all of available current digital services on the market is their heavy focus on the best places of a particular category. You can search for a best italian restaurant nearby OR for an art gallery, but never for a smooth trip including both. With our service you don't limit yourself to one type of places, because a good trip always combines different.

Prototype and testing

After on our analysis and our initial interviews, we decided to take one step ahead and test our ideas more concretely through prototyping. One of the most criteria which we wanted to clarify more was the respond and feeling of users to having different set of pre made profile.

Although, interviewees were interested in the idea of having pre set profile and choose the one which better fits there mood, some felt more comfortable to have access to a more personalized application, or choose number of different profiles at the same time.

Also through our testing and prototyping phase we got valuable feedback from potential users regarding different ways of planning the routs. in the following you can find some of our paper prototypes.

Solution

After considering all the feedback and results of our testing phase, we tried to tailor a new solution which covers all the problametic areas, in a way that makes Next stop not only desirable for tourists, but also for locals as well.

When you open NextStop, with one click you can choose the profile that describes the wishes for your trip. After that you see the top recommended routes around you. You can start exploring right away or save them for later. Or, if you want to narrow this down and find just the right hidden gems of the city, you can let us match you and your destinations. Rapid selection process lets you vote up or down for the suggested places and finally get a tailored set of places that fits your needs As soon as you have a route to explore, the application starts navigating you through it. You can choose public transportation that is considered to be the best option for tourists or walk if the distance is not too long.

Lokki

Lokki powered by F-Secure is a location based application, trying to offer new way of communication among family members. This project was part of Aalto Master studio and was done with 4 other team members.

The main goal in this project was to improve Lokki user experience and discovering new ways in which makes Lokki more desireable to be used among friends. We mostly focused on the user interface of Lokki and tried to redefine the ways users can use Lokki.

Initially we broke the whole redesiging process into 4 phases:

Pre-validation: In this phase, we used Lokki within our team for 1 week. During this week we tried to be committed using this application and kept record of our interactions and difficulties we faced using this application. After this period we developed patterns out of our usage records.

Initial assumptions: In this step we made some initial assumptions based on the feedbacks we got about the current drawbacks of Lokki application, we also brain stormed and had suggestion about new ways to use Lokki.

Validation: To test our assumptions, first we interviewed one family which were using Lokki. Although we got good responses from the family we also decided to test our ideas among potential users of Lokki to see what they would expect Lokki to offer to them?

Also two online surveys were created and distributed online, first one to get the actual feedback from Lokki users with insights on the possible ways of improving the application.

Second survey, was created for potential Lokki users in order to better understand the market need in different Lokki functions.

Solution: Here we proposed a new service design concept for Lokki application based on all the information we got during the interview and from surveys

After validating all the assumptions and taking all the insights into consideration, we came up with a new service design concept:

People need additional flexibility of the application. Therefore, it should be possible to create groups both around activities/places and around different categories of people. Besides that, users should have the possibility to customize sharing permissions for each group. It is also very important to provide users with an easy one-button functionality to switch sharing on or off. The new additional way of sharing is automatically updating status based on your location without explicitly showing where you are.

Lokki would be an Organized collection of the places, people and activities that matter

OPENAPI.fi

A joint project between Aalto University and OP Bank, to develope a new business model for OP bank. This project was done in a team of 7 master degree student of Aalto University and in close collaboration with OP bank professionals. In this project, I was the project manager of Aalto team.

In this project we explored the possibility of creating scalable and sustainable business models based on brokering information between consumers, merchants, service providers, financial institutions and public organizations – combining customer-specific purchase data from different sources to enable producing exceptional service experiences to the consumer, simultaneously creating measurable value to the ecosystem partner companies.

Our main goal here was to find and develop a win-win-win model;

For Customers: Wealth of available applications, and data protection

For Developers: User friendly, legal security, access to a wide market on international standards, possibility to sell

For Bank: Control over data access accelerate, leveraged and free R&D, extension of ecosystem

What We Did?

Our team developed a test open API, based on open banking standards designed to provide developers with a unified leading edge API to access financial data. Simple (REST) and effective methods to fetch data relating to customers, accounts, cards, transactions and balances with history, were consumed.

This was a one year long project, during which, we were challenged in different ways and pushed our boundaries ahead.

In September 2014, a hack&ideathon named openfin was organized by me, as a proof of concept for this project. The mail goal in OpenFin was not only to luanch our test open API, but also to validate the interest of external community of developers, service designers and creative minds to build and develope in the field of financial services.
